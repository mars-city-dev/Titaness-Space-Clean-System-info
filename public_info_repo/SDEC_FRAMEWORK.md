# SDEC Regulatory Framework
## Resolving Sovereignty through Environmental Intervention

The SDEC (Space Debris Excellence Consortium) framework provides the international community with a legal and technical path to orbital remediation that respects national jurisdiction while prioritizing environmental safety.

---

### 1. The Doctrine of "Protective Retrieval"
Under Article VIII of the 1967 Outer Space Treaty, nations retain "jurisdiction and control" over their space objects in perpetuity. To prevent cleanup from being classified as space piracy or an act of aggression, SDEC logs the capture of sovereign assets as a **Protective Retrieval**. 

*   **Mechanism:** Frames the action as an emergency environmental intervention, similar to the maritime duty to assist vessels in distress or remove hazardous wrecks.
*   **Legal Standing:** Shifts the burden of liability away from the "touching" party and onto the "protective" party acting on behalf of the consortium.

### 2. "Blind Verification" via Zero-Knowledge Proofs
A major barrier to cleanup is the fear that scrapper fleets will probe sensitive technology in "Spy Satellites." SDEC addresses this through:

*   **Zero-Knowledge Proofs (ZKP):** A cryptographic protocol where a retrieval unit can mathematically prove a container holds a specific mass from a specific launch without actually opening the vault or revealing its contents.
*   **Neutral Inspection:** International observers at neutral orbital outposts verify the seal and metadata of a **Titaness Container Unit (TCU)** without opening it.
*   **Salvage Fee:** Nations can pay a fee to have their sensitive technology returned in its sealed, un-probed box.

### 3. Abandonment Mechanism
To reduce global orbital liability, SDEC introduces a formalized **Abandonment Protocol**.
*   **Liability Reduction:** Nations can officially relinquish ownership of defunct assets to reduce their overall "Liability Score" within the consortium.
*   **Recycle Pending:** Assets are only moved to "Recycle Pending" status once they are officially cleared of sovereign status or formally abandoned by the owner.

### 4. Non-Aggression Protocol
To maintain international trust, the SDEC fleet operates under "Neutrality as Armor."
*   **Hardware Interlocks:** Guidance, Navigation, and Control (GNC) systems are hardware-locked to automatically abort if they approach any "Active Asset" flagged as "Non-Threatening."
*   **Intent Verification:** The fleet is technically incapable of offensive maneuvers against active sovereign infrastructure.
